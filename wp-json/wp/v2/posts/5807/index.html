Ode to a Tree
\nBy Eric Yu<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n

Amid the forest lies you, a tree,
\nA bridge to life
\nThat is a living organism,
\nBut your seeds will sprout into
\nThe new generations of trees ahead of you.
\nThey will feel the same
\nFeeling of luxurious sunlight that you have felt.
\nAnd as the seed sprouts,
\nIt grows into the world and clings
\nTo life from surviving natural disasters and a saw, and all
\nAround you
\nWere the divine symbols of hope,
\nThat was experienced with your same kind,
\nLike a school, but only changing through
\nSummer, spring, winter, and fall.
\nOnly you had provided the
\nWood, oxygen, and a home
\nThat many life-forms need.
\nYour apples put a smile on faces with their taste,
\nAnd the feeling of your branches make
\nChildren climb you
\nAnd as the wind passes by, people
\nStop to listen
\nWhhooooo (trees sound)
\nYou
\nWere like a purposeful form that
\nKeeps us from the suffocation
\nBy taking carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen.
\nThe breath of your oxygen makes our heart want to pump.
\nAnd your sprouting glory looks like a savior.
\nAnd as a bird flies by,
\nIt makes a home in your branches, that have endured a lot.
\nThe bird lays an egg, and its offspring grows
\nUntil the time is right
\nAnd it
\nFlies.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
