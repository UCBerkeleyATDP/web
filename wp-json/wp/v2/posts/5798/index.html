Ode to a Stone on the Shore
\nBy Joshua Morgan<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n

One day,
\nWandering, lost
\nAlong the shore,
\nI saw you
\nA stone whiter than sun-bleached bone and as smooth as the crystal
\nSky,
\nFloating in a sea of others.
\nYet somehow,
\nYou were different,
\nA gem,
\nSmoother and rounder,
\nFilled with a more
\nEthereal
\nEnergy
\nThan your rough and weary cousins.<\/p>\n

Wavy lines of ancient sediment cover you,
\nA beautiful,
\nMystic
\nDance of ages,
\nAn unkempt wave,
\nWeaving through space,
\nLeaping and swaying to
\nThe rhythmic
\nIn and out of the waves.
\nFilled with secrets to hold,
\nWitness to tales untold,
\nBut your mouth
\nIs tightly shut,
\nYour eyes
\nSewn closed,
\nYour ears
\nClamped shut,
\nYou
\nAre a heavy capsule of the knowledge of the ages.<\/p>\n

My hand encloses you, ripping you away
\nFrom your tide-pool
\nHome.
\nCrash!
\nThe ocean that caressed you shows its anger,
\nShows that it was to hold
\nAnd harrow
\nYou, wants you
\nTo heed
\nIts secrets and dreams.<\/p>\n

I can feel you quake,
\nBut I won\u2019t let it take you, not yet.
\nI peer
\nInto you
\nAnd will you to open yourself
\nAnd let me glimpse
\nYour stories, your secrets?
\nYet predictably
\nYou resist, like you have
\nFor so long.
\nBut still, eventually,
\nYour will gives way,
\nAnd I see,
\nI understand
\nThat you have seen time erode and
\nEmpires
\nFall.
\nI finally see that you,
\nA simple stone,
\nHave outlived me by so many millennium.
\nYou have existed
\nSince our sun was a newborn star,
\nEntrapped in the swirling
\nArms
\nOf dust and gas,
\nWhich would eventually become
\nYou and me.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
