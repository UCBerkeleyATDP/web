As you may already know, Nina Hersch Gabelko \u2013 ATDP\u2019s director for almost a quarter of a century \u2013 retired on October 1st and Adena Young has been named interim director. (In case you missed it, you can read\u00a0Adena\u2019s account of Nina\u2019s tenure<\/a>, which was published in\u00a0the final newsletter of Summer \u201911 [PDF, 1.7 Mb]<\/a>) As director, each year at student orientation, Nina has welcomed scores of ATDP students not to the \u201cprogram,\u201d<\/em>\u00a0and certainly not to \u201cclass<\/em>,\u201d but to an ongoing\u00a0conversation<\/em>\u00a0\u2013 a real one that fills the halls of every scholarly building on campus. As the ATDP staff look forward to 2012, now is an ideal time to review how Nina cultivated that conversation and how it is set to continue for years to come.<\/p>\n

Growing an Academic Community<\/strong><\/p>\n

At the end of the final day of ATDP\u2019s 2011 summer program, the Secondary Division faculty and staff filled a Tolman Hall meeting room, decked with streamers and balloons, in fond celebration of Nina\u2019s service. The results of Nina\u2019s legacy could not be clearer: everyone in the room spoke the same language of academic development and passion for teaching. They exchanged stories and ideas not only of student learning but \u2013 just as importantly \u2013 what they were learning as educators from their students and, of course, from Nina and the years-long conversation she has grown.<\/p>\n
