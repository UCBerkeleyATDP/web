Ode to the Acoustic Guitar On my Wall
\nby Tiffany Huynh<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n

Against the
\nBumpy cream wall of
\nMy bedroom,
\nYou are the full moon that
\nGlows against
\nNight, but with the soft
\nWarmth of the sun.<\/p>\n

I pick you up
\nMesmerized by your beauty and
\nStare, you are the well-known sculpture in a
\nFamous museum
\nYou are the
\nGentle shade of almond.
\nYour graceful long neck
\nIs the black top hat of a man in a wedding
\nYour six strings of strength and power
\nWrapped in a layer of golden rings and
\nYour great soul,
\nThat wooden box
\nIs what amplifies your song,
\nBecause it is hollow.<\/p>\n

I begin to pluck a slow tune.
\nYour voice smooth and polished like
\nYour dark chocolate shell
\nThe majestic tone of a composed queen addressing her people,
\nSinging without words, only meaning
\nand your lower notes, a lion\u2019s purr
\nBoom boom thump
\nAnd the higher notes clear
\nAs a shallow creek in the sun
\nRinging, and soft
\nLike the sweet sugar of a sponge cake
\nFreshly baked
\nThat leaves me full
\nFull of musical joy.<\/p>\n

We finish our magic
\nAnd I lean you against your piece of wall
\nYou are still
\nAs a sleeping rock
\nBut I have felt your heart beat
\nNo, you are not hollow
\nAnd no longer am I.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
