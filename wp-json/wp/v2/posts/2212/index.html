    We are proud to present the Fall\/Winter 2009<\/strong><\/span> issue of our seasonal eNewsletter. This issue is packed full of engaging articles, updates on new classes, and important information you’ll need for the application process and summer 2010.<\/p>\n

    How can one use the latest technology to observe and analyze classic works of art? And don’t forget to check out all our new offerings for summer 2010. It’s a year of many changes, but the ATDP promise of a challenging, enriching academic experience remains the same.<\/p>\n

    Going Greener, Growing Greener, Still<\/h3>\n

    Deep thanks to the hundreds upon hundreds of ATDP families who have already responded so positively in support of our effort to become greener. It will be a multi-year process because, just like everyone else, ATDP must accomplish as much as we can with our available resources and then gather the additional ones required to do more. Here is where we are for the coming summer:<\/p>\n

    Our online catalog<\/strong>
    \nFor 2010, we ask everyone to use the ATDP online catalog.<\/span> Please encourage your family, friends, and others who might be interested in attending ATDP to visit our main website,     atdp.berkeley.edu<\/strong><\/a>, where they will find both Secondary and Elementary Division catalogs and applications. The Summer 2010 materials will be available by early January 2010.<\/p>\n

    How to apply to ATDP<\/strong>
    \nTo help us prepare to go paperless (and save the office staff’s eyes), fill out your ATDP application on our website! To make sure we get everything right this first time around, we’ll still ask that you print your completed application after submitting it online and mail it in along with your other application documents (see Application Instructions for a complete list). If you apply this way, you’ll also be able to pay your application fee by credit card.<\/p>\n

    A very limited number of paper applications and catalogs will be available in the ATDP office for those who have no internet access at home, at school, or in their local public library.<\/p>\n

    A new way to pay<\/strong>
    \nFor the first time, ATDP is making available online, secure, payment by credit card. Instructions and options will be available as part of the 2010 application.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

    What will we be able to do in future years?<\/strong><\/h3>\n

    As soon as we can, we’ll be asking those of you applying online (and we hope there are many) to submit your entire applications electronically! For you, this means no photocopying, no stamps, and no trips to the post office. For us, this means less wasted paper, more accurate information, and faster processing! Look for these changes in the next few years. As always, we appreciate your help.<\/p>\n

    Catching up to the (fiscal) times<\/strong><\/h3>\n

    After 20+ years, we are forced to increase the application processing fee. Beginning Summer 2010, the fee will be $50. Information for financial aid applicants will be found in the application forms, online and in print.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
