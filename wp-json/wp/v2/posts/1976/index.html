Re-refashioning the Virtual ATDP
<\/strong>by Sam Pierce<\/em><\/div>\n

In order to make it easier to access information and resources (including fellow students and families), much of our effort has been spent on ATDP\u2019s online presence. Along with our usual course catalog, we\u2019re looking forward to rolling out a new website this winter. It\u2019ll look much like this eNewsletter and will help us all to realize more of the program\u2019s primary goals \u2013 to foster a productive and caring community of students, educators, and researchers, and lovers of learning.<\/p>\n

Last summer, I established a new version of our online academic component: the ATDP Commons. As is the tradition with our virtual class pages, it allowed students to communicate and coordinate with their peers and instructors during the program in a space that was all about the concepts and culture of their ATDP course. Going forward, we\u2019d like to integrate that social technology into our primary website, allowing instructors, parents, and others a forum to share stories, tips, and ideas related to the program year-round. When you log in, you\u2019ll be able to submit an application online, check its status, and make tuition payments.<\/p>\n

With this new setup, we\u2019ll continue to pursue lively and informative updates via our newsletter \u2013 look forward to hearing from us a bit more often, but please also consider contributing. In this issue, we\u2019ve got some wonderful anecdotes and advice from last summer\u2019s instructors<\/a>. In the future, we\u2019d love to feature student accomplishments, new developments in education, and program announcements. Please also let us know what you\u2019d like to see by commenting below!<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n

<\/p>\n

Sam Pierce<\/strong> is the program\u2019s webmaster and technical researcher. He is also a Secondary Division instructor during the summer, teaching ATDP\u2019s web design course.<\/em><\/p>\n
