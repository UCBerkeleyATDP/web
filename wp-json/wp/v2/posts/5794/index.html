Ode to the Red MP3
\nBy Nia Jones<\/em>
\n<\/strong>
\n Hear you playing
\nLike an orchestra.
\nYour strings, bass, percussion
\nIn each and every song come together
\nJust profoundly.
\nLike a great, big, musical family reunion.<\/p>\n

Your melodies gently brush
\nAgainst my music\u2013craving eardrums.
\nTheir heavy lust for your music
\nIs indescribable.
\nIt\u2019s not a want, no, it\u2019s definitely a need.
\nI guess you could say my ears
\nAre addicted.
\nAddicted to you like a drug.<\/p>\n

Sing to me, your music is medicine
\nTo my open emotional wounds.
\nThey heal my anger and sadness
\nLike a doctor. However you
\nDon\u2019t have a PhD, do you?<\/p>\n

Boom! Da-Boom!
\nThe bass in some of your music
\nBeats a steady rhythm
\nSomehow always in sync with my heart beat.
\nAnd as your tempo increases, my heart\u2019s reaction
\nIs
\nTo copy and thus match your speed.
\nAn unconscious body begins to tap along
\nAnd before I know it . . .
\nI\u2019m dancing!
\nMy friend, how do you have the
\nAbility
\nTo mind-control me like that?
\nHow do you take over my body
\nAs a puppeteer would?
\nBut however you do it,
\nDon\u2019t cease action!
\nI love the feeling it gives me.<\/p>\n

I pick and choose what
\nI want to listen to and
\nYou reply without any question, hesitation or doubt.
\nA lyrical servant, who caters to me.
\nOh! Have not I mentioned your tolerance?
\nNo even I, myself, would want to sit in dark trenches
\nWe humans call
\n\u201cPockets.\u201d
\nYet you spend most of your time in my trenches.
\nYou might as well call it
\nYour home.<\/p>\n

You, my one and only music player,
\nAre my life
\nBut who am
\nI
\nTo you?<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
