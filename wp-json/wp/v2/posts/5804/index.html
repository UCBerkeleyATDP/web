Ode to a Blade of Grass
\nBy Vasco Villas-Boas<\/em>
\n<\/strong>
\nI see you carefully tilting in the breeze
\nYou want to slide out of your roots (that won\u2019t give in) and be guided by the wind
\nAnd
\nBe free like a leaf floating downwards from a tree
\nYou are like a
\nHuman
\nThat wants to
\nJump out of a helicopter
\nAnd parachute down
\nTo see the
\nWorld<\/p>\n

When someone steps on you,
\nYou crumple on the ground and then
\nShiver
\nAnd then slowly rise up to your regular curved position
\nAnd the only way you\u2019ll be removed from the ground is by
\nA kid that wants to make a whistle in between his cupped hands,
\nA golfer who wants to know the direction of the wind so he can use the right club,
\nBy someone who just wants to feel your cool and silky yet sticky outer layer,
\nOr if you get mowed down<\/p>\n

You have the smell of a
\nLittle onion combined with an orange
\nWhich when I smell my mouth gives an overwhelmed
\n\u201cUrrhhhh\u201d
\nand when I like you, I feel you trying to take the
\nsaliva from my tongue
\n\u201cWuuuuu\u201d
\nI hear you blow past my ear
\nDo you really think you can get out of the ground?<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
