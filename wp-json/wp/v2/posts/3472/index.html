Each year, ATDP works to offer courses that will enrich students\u2019 interests and enhance their academic skills. This year, we are proud to introduce the following new courses for the Secondary Division<\/a>:<\/p>\n

Advanced Creative Writing<\/a> with Alex Franklin
\n Art History<\/a> with Laura Shefler *
\n Computer Engineering<\/a> with Abraham Liao*
\n Social Psychology<\/a> with David Chun*
\n Environmental Science<\/a> with Jonathan Wright<\/p>\n

What are these new classes really about, who\u2019s teaching them, and which one is right for you? In an exclusive series of interviews, three of our expert instructors share what drives them to teach, who will benefit from their classes, and why they\u2019re already itching to meet this summer\u2019s students.<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Logic and imagination intersect in Laura Shefler<\/strong>\u2019s introductory Art History<\/strong> course. Ms. Shefler welcomes art history buffs and novices alike to explore the links between past and present, ancient and contemporary\u2014and to rediscover and deepen their love of art.<\/p>\n

Read the interview here.<\/a><\/em><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Abraham Liao<\/strong> reflects on his journey from his time as an ATDP student obsessed with computer science to his current role as an ATDP instructor\u2014still obsessed with computer science. He also gives us a peek at his new course, Computer Engineering<\/strong>, in which students will discover how computers work from the bottom up.<\/p>\n

Read the interview here.<\/a><\/em><\/p>\n

Can you get someone to agree with a majority opinion\u2014even if that opinion is blatantly wrong? David Chun<\/strong> details how his future students will put this theory (and many others) to the test when they investigate the inner workings of people in Social Psychology<\/strong>.<\/p>\n
