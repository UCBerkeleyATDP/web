Ode to my Racket
\nBy Vanessa Van Pell<\/em>
\n<\/strong>
\nYou are
\nNervous
\nYour strings tighten
\nAs I warm up<\/p>\n

I feel the blood start to pump
\nThrough every vein in my body
\nYou start to settle
\nKnowing I will protect you<\/p>\n

I will not let you down
\nAll the nights you stayed up
\nJust to let me play an extra 380 balls
\nEvery sound as crisp as a
\nButcher cut<\/p>\n

Then we start
\nI grip hard to feel your gold
\nThe black
\nI had hope<\/p>\n

I see you as a shooting star
\nSomething I will always wish on,
\nBut only a few times does it turn out
\nMy way<\/p>\n

I smell the sweat
\nSlowly dripping down
\nMy hands onto you
\nUnlike others you will not
\nSlip out of my hands<\/p>\n

The terror that everyone looks at you with
\nPraying that Alex strung you well
\nYou are dark,
\nDevious
\nNot to me though.<\/p>\n

The smell of water
\nLinger over the dark brown hair
\nOver my head
\nI would pull through for all you have
\nDone for me<\/p>\n

I know that you are nervous
\nSo nervous,
\nYet not once
\nAre you trembling with the wind.
\n6-4 5-3 and I\u2019m up,
\nand I need one more point to win this,
\njust one<\/p>\n

I glare hard down the sun-burnt courts
\nYes I wish on you,
\nNo one will ever know what I say to you
\nNow you don\u2019t go and tell people
\nIt\u2019s our little secret<\/p>\n

The salt on my face trickles down,
\nInto the dried creases
\nOf my mouth
\nI had won,
\nI grinned and looked at you
\nI knew you had given it your all
\nLoses or wins
\nI don\u2019t care
\n\u2018Cause I had you<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
