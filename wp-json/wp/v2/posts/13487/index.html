Good morning everyone! \u00a0Glad you decided to wake up so early. \u00a0It\u2019s nice to see so many people here.<\/span><\/p>\n

Since senioritis has hit its apex for me, and I\u2019ve procrastinated on writing this speech until this week, I\u2019ve decided that I\u2019ll have you guys do some of the work. \u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

So, how many of you know exactly, exactly what you want to do in college, or after college, you know, as a chosen career path? \u00a0Or life plan? \u00a0Let\u2019s get a show of hands.<\/span><\/p>\n

For me, it wasn\u2019t quite so easy. \u00a0Way back in 2010, when Toy Story 3 came out, Spain won the World Cup, and Simon left American Idol, I (too) had absolutely no idea what I was going to do when I was older. \u00a0To be fair, I had only just finished sixth grade, but because I had just finished the sixth grade, my parents insisted that I \u201cdo something productive\u201d over the summer. \u00a0I was not particularly\u2026 enthused with that prospect. \u00a0Yeah, I was that kid, and yeah, I know that\u2019s some of you out there.<\/span><\/p>\n

However, they gave me the option to pick among the courses in the catalog. \u00a0So I picked the most interesting one to me: the introductory web design course, The Internet Classroom. \u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Ok, to be perfectly honest, it looked like the least boring one. \u00a0Funny thing is though, that upon entering the class, and perhaps even a little leading up to, I got super into what I was doing. \u00a0<\/span>After that year, I kept coming back, and then, I really was picking the most interesting classes. \u00a0And one year, one became something more. \u00a0That class was Computer Engineering, and I instantly knew that this was the kind of stuff I wanted to do, and so I pursued it. \u00a0You would be hard pressed to find a person my age who gets excited about XOR gates and truth tables. \u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

That was when it happened. \u00a0I found what I liked to do, and I continued to pursue it. \u00a0I returned year after year as a TA, and this is my 7th year with ATDP\u2019s computer science department. \u00a0And next year, when I go to college at UCLA, I\u2019ll be studying Electrical Engineering.<\/span><\/p>\n

But what\u2019s important is this. \u00a0ATDP is an outlet for you to explore what you might want to do, what you like to do. \u00a0You might not find what you like to do now, or it may change in the future. \u00a0But you just might. \u00a0It might be that Japanese class you keep going back to every year, first as a student, then as a teacher\u2019s assistant. \u00a0It might be that one English class you take after two years of math classes. \u00a0Heh, it might be the one Math class you take after nothing but English classes.<\/span><\/p>\n

My point is this: make the most out of your classes. \u00a0If you like it, learn more about it. \u00a0Explore your passions. \u00a0If you don\u2019t, come back next year and pick a different course. \u00a0Rinse and repeat.<\/span><\/p>\n

Do I know what exactly exactly I want to do when I grow up? \u00a0Probably not. \u00a0But after my experience here, I think I have a better idea.
\n<\/span>
