Welcome!<\/em><\/h1>\n

ATDP remains committed to helping you make the most of your summer. This page contains comprehensive information about our Secondary Division programs for 2023.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

\n
\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

Rigorous coursework<\/h3>\n

Secondary Division courses can help meet a diverse variety of scholastic goals. Some aim to help middle school students to transition to high school topics, or high school students to college-level study. Others are equivalent to full-year high school or Advanced Placement curricula, offering the potential for students to accelerate through more advanced levels in a subject.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

Robust engagement<\/h3>\n

We’ve always united teachers who are passionate about their subject with students who love to learn. Whether in-person or online, class sessions are highly interactive and aim to maximize the value of every minute. Online classes will include a mix of live (“synchronous”) and self-directed (“asynchronous”) instruction and activity.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

A community of learners<\/h3>\n

ATDP courses aren’t just about the curriculum. Every summer, students come from far and wide to share their interests with one another, work in class together, and make new friends. In a time of social distancing, it is all the more important that we find ways to maintain students’ opportunities for connection.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Courses <\/i><\/a><\/h1>\n
        \n

        Browse the online course catalog  <\/i><\/strong><\/a><\/p>\n

        Unlike many other summer programs, ATDP offers courses that focus on acquisition of deep knowledge in a single topic. Secondary Division students are invited to immerse themselves and gain expertise in a subject that interests them. Some ATDP classes are designed to accelerate students through high school curricula. Many ATDP courses now count toward UC and CSU college entrance requirements.<\/p>\n

        Choosing a Course <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        With nearly 40 different courses to choose from, it is sometimes difficult for students to find the one course that is just right for them! We encourage you to read through the course descriptions<\/a> to carefully identify a course that interests you and for which you are academically prepared. It is important to note course prerequisites and grade level requirements when you are selecting your course choices, as not all courses are open to all students. In addition to selecting one course that you are most interested in taking, you may select up to three other courses as alternatives.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

         <\/p>\n

        \n
        Course Load <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        An ATDP course requires a serious time commitment. Students should expect to spend between three and ten hours preparing for each class meeting. The average number of hours of self-study to expect per class meeting is provided in each course description and new students especially should expect to be spending time on homework that is at the upper end of the range provided.<\/p>\n

        <\/i> Half-year equivalent courses<\/strong> cover about a semester of course content in six weeks. The pace is accelerated but not rushed. Some of these courses are excellent options for current middle school students (including those continuing from the Elementary Division) who seek an introduction to high school-level topics. Others feature unique or special topics suitable for high schoolers preparing to transition to college; these advanced courses typically have academic prerequisites.<\/p>\n

        <\/i> Full-year equivalent<\/strong> courses cover the equivalent of two semesters of course content in six weeks. The pace is extremely fast. Students should expect to dedicate a large amount of time to homework and self-study outside of class, and we recommend that they schedule no other summer activities during the program. Most of these courses have academic prerequisites.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

         <\/p>\n

        \n
        Schedule & Size <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        ATDP offers courses that meet either two or three days each week. Most run the full six weeks of the Secondary Division (June 20 \u2013 July 28), though a small number run on unique four-week schedules.<\/p>\n

        Classes generally have a cap of 20 to 24 students. The low teacher-to-student ratio, as well as the academic and administrative staff support, ensure instruction of the highest quality.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        \n
        Types of Courses <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tA\u2013G Approved Courses<\/i><\/i>
        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

        If approved to count towards UC\/CSU admission requirements, each course lists its UC\/CSU A-G subject. Many high school-level ATDP courses are intended for college preparation (G). The online course catalog<\/a> will list any updates as ATDP courses are approved throughout the year. For ATDP courses currently listed as \u201cin process,\u201d approval will be retroactive and will include this summer. Only A-G courses taken after the beginning of 9th grade\u2014with the exception of math and LOTE\/Language Other Than English\u2014can be reported on the UC application<\/a>. (Courses designed exclusively for students completing 7th and 8th grade are not eligible for A-G approval.)<\/p>\n

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tUC Honors Designation<\/i><\/i>
        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

        Due to the pace and depth of the curricula, all<\/em> ATDP courses for high school students are taught at what most schools would list as \u201chonors\u201d level. This is different from the UC honors designation, which is an optional part of each course\u2019s A-G status. Courses designated by UC as \u201chonors\u201d are comparable to introductory college-level courses in the subject, and always include a culminating final exam or project.<\/p>\n

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tAP-aligned Courses<\/i><\/i>
        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

        AP-aligned courses are especially rigorous full-year courses designed to help prepare students for an Advanced Placement exam in the subsequent spring. Take special note of these courses\u2019 prerequisites and homework as well as self-study time required per class meeting. Review during the school year is highly recommended, and additional self-study may be required for full exam preparation. Students who pass the AP exam may be eligible for college credit, and some AP exam scores may be counted toward the UC\/CSU A-G subject requirement.<\/p>\n

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tChoosing a Writing Course<\/i><\/i>
        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

        Applicants sometimes have trouble figuring out which writing course is most appropriate for their skill level, age, and grade. Aside from creative writing, all of our writing courses share very similar learning goals, just at different levels. We recommend including all acceptable writing course alternatives on your application form. Based on your academic product, grade, age, and Letter of Interest, we will place you in the most appropriate class with other students of similar preparedness.<\/p>\n

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tChoosing a Math Course<\/i><\/i>
        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

        Accelerated core math courses are intensive full-year courses that are equivalent to the traditional high school math sequence, and are recommended only for students seeking acceleration at their school. They have rigid prerequisites and a special application process. Please review the special requirements and instructions for applying to these courses<\/a>. <\/p>\n

        Other math courses are designed to help students who want to gain a deeper understanding of specialized math topics and who are not seeking acceleration at their school.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Applying for Two Courses <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Only students with strong academic backgrounds are allowed to take two courses, and new Secondary Division students (including those who previously attended ATDP\u2019s Elementary Division<\/a>) are less likely to be allowed to enroll in two courses. Remember that expectations are high in every ATDP course, and many former students have reported that taking two courses was more time-consuming than they expected. Add together the two courses\u2019 estimated hours per week listed in the course catalog<\/a> for an approximate time commitment.<\/p>\n

        If you are interested in enrolling in two courses, you must petition to do so in your Letter of Interest that accompanies your application. The petition should explain your summer plan: clearly identify your desired schedule of two courses, making sure their meeting times do not conflict, as well as any alternatives (single courses or other two-course combinations) in order of preference. Also describe how you plan to manage time commitments outside of class meetings. Successful petitions tend to be for two half-year equivalent courses, or one full-year equivalent course with a smaller time commitment and one half-year course.<\/p>\n

        Please also consider Explorations<\/a> if your goal is to plan a full day of activity. Students enrolled in a Secondary Division course can register for Explorations without applying separately.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Acceptance Notification <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Students who have been accepted to ATDP and who applied by the early or standard postmark deadlines will receive their acceptance notification in their online account and via email approximately one month later (see the program calendar<\/a>).         \n
        Confirming Enrollment <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        To confirm course placement, a parent or guardian of a student who plans to attend must complete the online Enrollment Form\u2014which includes emergency information, policy agreement, and tuition payment\u2014by the May 9 tuition deadline.<\/p>\n

        The policy agreement will include our safety plan for 2023, which we will calibrate in response to evolving public health conditions, guidelines, and UC Berkeley policies. Agreeing to these policies is a condition of enrollment.<\/p>\n

        Total cost for each ATDP course can be found under the individual course\u2019s description. This includes the base tuition fee and program fee, but does not include the application processing fee, nor expenses for textbooks, supplies, or transportation.<\/p>\n

        Sibling Discount<\/h5>\n

        For families confirming enrollment for two or more siblings, a $35 tuition discount is available for each student. For example, if two siblings attended, each student would receive one $35 discount from tuition, for a total family discount of $70. This discount is taken from the course’s base tuition only, not from the application fee or the program fee. <\/p>\n

        Refunds<\/h5>\n

        A parent or guardian whose registered student is unable to attend must withdraw the student in writing and may request a tuition refund prior to Wed., May 17, 2023. No refund will be made in the case of a student who fails to attend classes or is withdrawn from ATDP for failure to meet the standards of appropriate behavior, including completion of homework. See our refund policy<\/a>.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Getting to Class<\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Secondary Division courses will be offered as part of two programs: one in-person on the UC Berkeley campus and one remote through live online meetings (Zoom). Choose your preferred format at the time of application.<\/p>\n

        In-person classes<\/h5>\n

        All SD in-person courses are held on the UC Berkeley campus, which is conveniently located a short walk away from the Downtown Berkeley BART station and several AC Transit bus lines. Many families choose to carpool to campus, and information about carpools, BART meet-ups, and campus shuttles will be provided in the student\u2019s acceptance materials. For families driving to Berkeley, please visit UC Berkeley\u2019s Parking and Transportation website<\/a> for parking details.<\/p>\n

        Remote learning classes<\/h5>\n

        All SD remote learning courses are held live, usually on Zoom, and attendance and participation expectations are the same as for in-person courses. Students will need to have a stable internet connection of sufficient speed, a computer capable of joining Zoom meetings, and a camera. ATDP remote classes have a program-wide \u201ccameras on\u201d policy, so students should be prepared for live interactions with instructors and classmates. Course descriptions for online courses may include additional information about that course\u2019s particular remote format.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Attendance <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        There are no excused absences at ATDP.<\/em> As expectations are high and courses are fast-paced, even one absence makes it difficult to keep pace. Therefore, students are expected to attend every class session. Missing too much class time for any reason may result in dismissal from the program without refund.<\/em> See our attendance policy<\/a>.<\/p>\n

          The Application Process <\/i><\/a><\/h3>\n

          \n\t\t\t\tATDP evaluates applications on an ongoing basis in the order they are received, beginning when an application is marked complete. The earlier you submit a complete application, the better your chances at receiving placement into your preferred course!\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

            \n
          • The application process is the same for new and returning students.<\/li>\n
          • Students must reapply every year, and returning students are not guaranteed automatic readmission or placement in their first choice of course.<\/li>\n
          • Incomplete applications are not considered for placement.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

            \n\t\t\t\tAm I a new or returning student?<\/i><\/i>
            \n\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

            A student who applied in a previous year and then withdrew without completing a course will be considered a new student for admission purposes. Students who have attended the Elementary Division previously and are applying to the Secondary Division for the first time are also considered new students, but their previous participation is noted.<\/p>\n

            \n\t\t\t\tContinuing from Elementary Division to Secondary Division<\/i><\/i>
            \n\t\t\t<\/h4>\n

            Many students return summer after summer, taking advanced courses they cannot take at their regular schools. ATDP is committed to serving returning students through their elementary, middle, and high school years.<\/p>\n

            \t\t\tFormer Elementary Division students applying to the Secondary Division must demonstrate continued academic achievement as well as good work habits and citizenship. While participation in previous summers\u2019 Elementary Division is acknowledged, it is important for students and their families to remember that readmission to ATDP is never automatic, and the academic requirements for SD courses are increased relative to ED.<\/p>\n

                  Instructions <\/i><\/a><\/h3>\n

                  Step 1: Choose a Course <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

                  Browse our course offerings<\/a>, then choose one course that interests you and up to three alternates. Make note of any grade requirements or prerequisites. Keep our attendance policy<\/a> in mind as you review the course schedule. See the Courses<\/a> section above for more information about differences between the large variety of SD courses.<\/p>\n

                  All courses run during the six weeks of June 20\u2013July 28, 2023 unless otherwise noted.<\/p>\n

                  Course availability may change throughout the application season as courses fill. Check our list of course offerings<\/a> for frequent updates.<\/p>\n

                  Step 2: Prepare Required Materials <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

                  Please prepare the following items in the order listed below. Items numbered 1 through 6 are required for a complete application. We request you include College Board PSAT\/SAT scores (item 7) if they are available, and federal tax documentation (item 8) if you wish to apply for financial aid.<\/p>\n

                  Items 1-7 can be submitted in the online application as a PDF (recommended) or image file. Due to campus policy, tax documents (item 8) must be provided as a paper copy via fax or mail.<\/p>\n

                    \n
                  1. \n

                    \n\t\t\t\t\t\t1. Application Fee <\/i><\/i>
                    \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                    \n

                    There is an application fee for each prospective student. The fee is $50 for domestic students. For students attending school outside the US, the fee is $80. This fee is non-refundable. It covers only the cost of application processing and does not apply toward tuition or facilities fees. Available payment methods include:<\/p>\n

                      \n
                    • Online payment by credit\/debit card (preferred)<\/strong><\/li>\n
                    • Check or money order made payable to \u201cUC Regents.\u201d Write \u201cSD\u201d and the student\u2019s first and last name on the memo line. Mail your check payment to ATDP (see \u201cMailing Instructions\u201d below).\n
                      Mailed payments may delay your application. We strongly recommend making your payment online if at all possible.<\/div>\n<\/li>\n
                    • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\tThose applying for need-based financial aid may apply for an application fee waiver if the fee poses a financial hardship. See our Financial Aid page<\/a> for details.\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/li>\n<\/ul><\/div>\n<\/li>\n
                    • \n

                      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t2. Letter of Interest <\/i><\/i>
                      \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                      \n

                      Please write a cover letter to accompany your application, 200 word minimum, explaining your reasons for choosing each of the courses listed on your application. In your (the student\u2019s) own words, include information about your interest in the subject(s), what you hope to learn, and related experience, if any. If the course(s) you list have prerequisites, mention how you have met them. In this letter, only discuss courses in which you are actually interested in enrolling. Remember, this letter should be about YOU and your personal interest, goals, and qualifications, not about the relevance of the course(s) in general.<\/strong><\/p>\n

                      You or your parent or guardian may also append any written notes at the end of your letter if you wish to have them considered as part of your application. This may include any special circumstances, such as transportation or scheduling needs, for example. <\/p>\n

                      If you are applying for two courses<\/a>, include your petition in your letter. Specify your desired course schedule, and explain your plan for managing the increased time commitment.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/li>\n

                    • \n

                      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t3. Teacher Referral Form <\/i><\/i>
                      \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                      \n

                      ATDP\u2019s Teacher Referral Form (TRF) is a brief online form that collects feedback from your teacher, and must be completed as part of your online application before it can be submitted. It must be completed by a current teacher that instructs in any academic subject (e.g., mathematics, science, language arts; not elective, advisory, or extracurricular classes).<\/p>\n

                      The teacher\u2019s academic subject need not match the subject of the ATDP course(s) to which you are applying, with one exception. If you are applying for Algebra I<\/em>, Geometry<\/em>, Algebra II\/Trigonometry<\/em>, or Precalculus<\/em> this form must be completed by your current math teacher (see all accelerated core mathematics prerequisites<\/a>).<\/p>\n

                      Remember that your teacher\u2019s time is valuable. Ask your teacher ahead of time before you send them an online request. We recommend sending your teacher this form at least one week before you plan to submit it with your application.<\/p>\n

                      You have two options for requesting that your teacher complete it:<\/p>\n

                        \n
                      • Identify your teacher in your online application (preferred)<\/em>.<\/strong> We\u2019ll email your teacher a link to an online form for them to complete. Note that you won\u2019t be able to submit your online application until your teacher completes the form.
                        or<\/strong>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/li>\n
                      • Send your teacher a request before applying online.<\/strong> You can request a Teacher Referral Form early by visiting atdp.berkeley.edu\/trf\/student<\/a> and providing your teacher\u2019s information. We\u2019ll email your teacher a link to an online form for them to complete. Once they\u2019re done, we\u2019ll email you a code that you can use to add the completed form to an online application. (Teachers can also send referrals to students that they feel would be a good fit for our program!)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tDo not request a letter of recommendation. Do not submit more than one Teacher Referral Form with your application materials.<\/strong>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/li>\n

                      • \n

                        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t4. Copy of Report Card <\/i><\/i>
                        \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                        \n

                        Submit a legible copy of your first semester (or most recent) report card for the current (\u201922\u2013\u201923) school year.<\/p>\n

                        We request that you send final grades, but if those are not available, you may submit your most recent progress report. Do not delay submitting your application to wait for final grades; if necessary, we may ask for an updated report card after receiving your application. If you need assistance obtaining a copy, contact your school office.<\/p>\n

                        Screenshots of online reports are only acceptable if they clearly include the student’s name and date of the grades displayed, and we may ask for an updated report card after receiving your application.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/li>\n

                      • \n

                        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t5. Copy of Achievement Test Scores <\/i><\/i>
                        \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                        \n

                        Submit a legible copy of your most recent California standardized test (CAASPP) or other standardized achievement test scores (e.g., Stanford Achievement Test, Iowa Test of Basic Skills, or other school-administered test that gives national percentile scores). The test must have been taken within the past three years (i.e., 2022, 2021, or 2020) and include scores in the areas of math and reading\/ELA. SAT\/PSAT scores do not satisfy this required item, but may also be submitted optionally (see item 7).<\/p>\n

                        Note:<\/strong> Since CAASPP testing did not occur in 2020, most California public school students should submit the CAASPP scores they received in 2021 or 2022. Students currently in 10th or 11th grade may submit a self-signed note if they have not taken a standardized achievement test in the past three years.<\/p>\n

                        Do not delay submitting your application to wait for more recent test scores. Include whatever acceptable test scores you have from the last three years.<\/p>\n

                        If you have not taken a standardized achievement test in the past three years, please obtain and submit a note from an administrative staff person at your school indicating so.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/li>\n

                      • \n

                        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t6. Academic Product or Essay <\/i><\/i>
                        \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                        \n

                        Please submit an academic product that meets the criteria of one of the following three options (A, B or C). This work should be one of which you are especially proud and which was completed since September 2022. While the work may have been done for a school assignment, it need not have been. The product you submit need not be in the same subject area as that to which you are applying, but keep in mind that the written component must develop your own original thinking beyond restating facts.<\/p>\n

                        Option A – Existing Essay or Story<\/h5>\n

                        Submit an essay or story of at least 500 words that shows your original thought and that is long enough for you to develop your ideas. A social studies or science report is not appropriate unless it relies heavily on your own analysis and discussion in addition to reporting factual information.<\/p>\n

                        Option B – Other Existing Product<\/h5>\n

                        If you\u2019d like to submit a piece of academic work that deviates from a traditional essay or story format (e.g., art, poetry, computer programs, or science experiments), you must also submit a clear, well-developed explanation of your work. Your written explanation should be at least 500 words and must be long enough to clearly demonstrate your thinking as you developed this product.<\/p>\n

                        Option C – Write an Essay<\/h5>\n

                        Write a well-developed essay of between 500 and 1,500 words on one of the topics below. Take time to consider the topic in depth and organize your answer. Be sure to title your essay. Save your essay as a PDF document so you can upload it to your application (on some computers, you may need to use the Print function).<\/p>\n

                          \n
                        • Write about someone you’ve only met one time but still remember. To meet or exceed the minimum word count, compose a narrative with a beginning, middle, and conclusion, and which contains descriptive details. (Embellishment is okay if you can’t remember all the particulars.) What was the encounter like? Why has the memory stuck with you? What meanings have you taken away from it?<\/li>\n
                        • Artificial intelligence (AI) has many uses today, but it is not without controversy, as seen in the recent debates around the use of AI to create art. Make and defend a claim: what is one job or task that AI should be used to do? Or conversely, what is one job or task AI should not be used to do? Be sure to organize your essay with relevant evidence and reasoning.<\/li>\n<\/ul><\/div>\n<\/li>\n
