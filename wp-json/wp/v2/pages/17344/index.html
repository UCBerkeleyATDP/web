Welcome!<\/em><\/h1>\n

ATDP remains committed to helping you make the most of your summer. This page contains comprehensive information about our Elementary Division programs for 2023.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

\n
\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

Deep knowledge acquisition<\/h3>\n

Each Elementary Division course offers young students the opportunity to become budding experts in a chosen topic. Whether focusing on a specific academic subject or building skills more broadly, each engaging curriculum focuses on learning in depth and having fun!<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

Robust engagement<\/h3>\n

We’ve always united teachers who are passionate about their subject with students who love to learn. Whether in-person or online, class sessions are highly interactive and aim to maximize the value of every minute. Online classes include a healthy balance of live on-screen interaction with instructors and classmates, activities that are self-directed or parent-assisted, and plenty of screen breaks.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

A community of learners<\/h3>\n

ATDP courses aren’t just about the curriculum. Every summer, students come from far and wide to share their interests with one another, work in class together, and make new friends. In a time of social distancing, it is all the more important that we find ways to maintain students’ opportunities for connection.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Schedule & Workload <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Classes meet within a three and a half hour window. For online classes, the amount of live (synchronous) time may be less. Morning classes are from 8:30 a.m. to noon, and afternoon classes are from 1:00 to 4:30 p.m. Schedules will include recess\/screen breaks. For estimated maximum homework times, see each individual course description in the online catalog.<\/p>\n

        Students completing Grades 4 to 6 with strong academic records are allowed to enroll in a second academic class if there is space. Any invitation to enroll in a second class is made available with the acceptance notification.<\/p>\n

        Please also consider Explorations if your goal is to plan a full day or week of activity. Students enrolled in a Elementary Division course can register for Explorations without applying separately.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Choosing a Course <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Each course will concentrate on one subject area, such as The Art and Science of Math<\/i> (for 4th graders) or Human Physiology<\/i> (for 6th graders). Please carefully review the course descriptions for your grade level.<\/p>\n

        We generally recommend that parents discuss all the course options with their child and that they choose together the class that best capitalizes on the child\u2019s academic strengths and deep interests. The subject chosen should be one that the student will want to learn about for three weeks and explore in depth with hands-on projects and interesting activities.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Acceptance Notification <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Students who have been accepted to ATDP and who applied by the early or standard postmark deadlines will receive their acceptance notification in their online account and via email approximately one month later (see the program calendar<\/a>). This notification will come along with our ED Student & Family guide, which contains important information and instructions for confirming your enrollment and preparing to attend class.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        \n
        Confirming Enrollment <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        To confirm course placement, a parent or guardian of a student who plans to attend must complete the online Enrollment Form\u2014which includes emergency information, policy agreement, and tuition payment\u2014by the May 25 tuition deadline.<\/p>\n

        The policy agreement will include our safety plan for 2023, which we will calibrate in response to evolving public health conditions, guidelines, and UC Berkeley policies. Agreeing to these policies is a condition of enrollment.<\/p>\n

        Total cost for each ATDP course can be found under the individual course\u2019s description. This includes the base tuition fee, program fee, and (if applicable) supply kit, but does not<\/strong> include the application processing fee or expenses for basic supplies on the shopping lists provided by a few online instructors.<\/p>\n

        Sibling Discount<\/h5>\n

        For families confirming enrollment for two or more siblings, a $35 tuition discount is available for each student. For example, if two siblings attended, each student would receive one $35 discount from tuition, for a total family discount of $70. This discount is taken from the course’s base tuition only, not from the application fee or the program fee. <\/p>\n

        Refunds<\/h5>\n

        A parent whose registered student is unable to attend must withdraw the student in writing and may request a tuition refund prior to Wed., June 7, 2023. No refund will be made in the case of a student who fails to attend classes or is withdrawn from ATDP for failure to meet the standards of appropriate behavior, including completion of homework. See our refund policy<\/a>.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Getting to Class<\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        Elementary Division courses are offered through two different programs: one in-person at Washington School in Pt. Richmond and one remote through live online meetings (Zoom). Choose your preferred format at the time of application.<\/p>\n

        In-person classes<\/h5>\n

        All ED in-person courses are held at Washington Elementary School, located at 565 Wine Street in Point Richmond, nine miles northwest of UC Berkeley. The historic small town of Point Richmond is nestled in a quiet area between Interstate 580 and San Francisco Bay, near the eastern anchorage of the Richmond-San Rafael Bridge. Many families choose to carpool, and information about our automated commuter directory will be provided in the student\u2019s acceptance materials. <\/p>\n

        For families that want a full day of activity on site, see also Cal Adventures Sports & Arts Camp<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Remote learning classes<\/h5>\n

        All ED remote learning courses are held live on Zoom, and attendance and participation expectations are similar in-person courses. Students will need to have a stable internet connection of sufficient speed, a computer capable of joining Zoom meetings, and a camera. ATDP remote classes have a program-wide \u201ccameras on\u201d policy, so students should be prepared for live interactions with instructors and classmates. Course descriptions for online courses contain additional information about that course\u2019s particular remote format, including how much parental involvement may be required.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        Attendance <\/i><\/a><\/div>\n
        \n
        \n

        There are no excused absences at ATDP.<\/em> As expectations are high and courses are fast-paced, even one absence makes it difficult to keep pace. Therefore, students are expected to attend every class session. Missing too much class time for any reason may result in dismissal from the program without refund.<\/em> See our attendance policy<\/a>.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/p><\/div>\n

        The Application Process <\/i><\/a><\/h3>\n

        \n\t\t\t\tATDP evaluates applications on an ongoing basis in the order they are received, beginning when an application is marked complete. The earlier you submit a complete application, the better your chances at receiving placement into your preferred course!\n\t\t\t<\/p>\n

                    \n\t\t\t\t\t\t2. Teacher Referral Form <\/i><\/i>
                    \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                    \n

                    ATDP\u2019s Teacher Referral Form (TRF) is a brief online form that collects feedback from your teacher, and must be completed as part of your online application before it can be submitted. It must be completed by the student\u2019s current primary teacher or another current teacher who instructs in any academic subject (e.g., mathematics, science, language arts; not<\/strong> elective, advisory, or extracurricular classes). The teacher\u2019s academic subject need not match the subject of the ATDP course(s) to which you are applying. <\/p>\n

                    Remember that your teacher\u2019s time is valuable. Ask your teacher ahead of time before send them an online request. We recommend sending your teacher this form at least one week before you plan to submit it with your application.<\/p>\n

                    You have two options for requesting that your teacher complete it:<\/p>\n

                      \n
                    • Identify your teacher in your online application (preferred)<\/em>.<\/strong> We\u2019ll email your teacher a link to an online form for them to complete. Note that you won\u2019t be able to submit your online application until your teacher completes the form.
                      or<\/strong>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/li>\n
                    • Send your teacher a request before applying online.<\/strong> You can request a Teacher Referral Form early by visiting atdp.berkeley.edu\/trf\/student<\/a> and providing your teacher\u2019s information. We\u2019ll email your teacher a link to an online form for them to complete. Once they\u2019re done, we\u2019ll email you a code that you can use to add the completed form to an online application. (Teachers can also send referrals to students that they feel would be a good fit for our program!)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tDo not request a letter of recommendation. Do not submit more than one Teacher Referral Form with your application materials.<\/strong>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/li>\n

                      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t3. Copy of Report Card <\/i><\/i>
                      \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                      \n

                      Submit a legible copy of your first semester (or most recent) report card for the current (\u201922\u2013\u201923) school year.<\/p>\n

                      We request that you send final grades, but if those are not available, you may submit your most recent progress report. Do not delay submitting your application to wait for final grades; if necessary, we may ask for an updated report card after receiving your application. If you need assistance obtaining a copy, contact your school office.<\/p>\n

                      If your school does not issue grades or marks, please submit whatever kind of progress report is provided.<\/p>\n

                      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t4. Copy of Achievement Test Scores <\/i><\/i>
                      \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                      \n

                      Students in grade 2 and below may skip this item. Students in grade 3 may skip this item if they have not taken any achievement tests.<\/p>\n

                      Submit a legible copy of your most recent California standardized test (CAASPP) or other standardized achievement test scores (e.g., Stanford Achievement Test, Iowa Test of Basic Skills, or other school-administered test that gives national percentile scores). The test must have been taken within the past three years (i.e., 2022, 2021, or 2020) and include scores in the areas of math and reading\/ELA.<\/p>\n

                      Note:<\/strong> Since CAASPP testing did not occur in 2020, most California public school students should submit the CAASPP scores they received in 2021 or 2022.<\/p>\n

                      Do not delay submitting your application to wait for more recent test scores. Include whatever acceptable test scores you have from the last three years.<\/p>\n

                      If you have not taken a standardized achievement test in the past three years, please mention it at the end of your Statement of Interest.<\/p>\n<\/p><\/div>\n<\/li>\n

                      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t5. Academic Product or Essay <\/i><\/i>
                      \n\t\t\t\t\t<\/h4>\n
                      \n

                      Please submit a product written in English and of grade-appropriate length. As a rough guide:<\/p>\n

                        \n
                      • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\tKindergarten to Grade 1: 3-5 sentences\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/li>\n
                      • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\tGrade 2 to Grade 4: 2-3 paragraphs\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/li>\n
                      • \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\tGrade 5 to Grade 6: 1-2 pages\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
                        Option A – Existing Essay or Story<\/h5>\n

                        Submit a piece of original written work that shows your own original thinking. This work should be one of which you are especially proud and which was completed since September 2022. While the work may have been done for a school assignment, it need not have been. The product you submit need not be in the same subject area to which you are applying. Examples of appropriate products include English essays, social studies reports, and poetry. (Poems must include a paragraph explaining the meaning behind the words.) Examples of unacceptable products include drawings not accompanied by original writing, spelling tests, arithmetic exercises, slideshow presentations, fill-in-the-blank worksheets, and multiple choice tests.
