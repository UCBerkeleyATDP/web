Course Placement<\/h1>\n
\n
\n
\n

Course placement details, including any wait pool status, can be viewed on your online account by logging into atdp.berkeley.edu\/account<\/a> and going to the COURSE PLACEMENT tab. We attempt to place students in their first choice when possible. Factors in course placement include the date an application is complete<\/em> (not necessarily the date submitted<\/em>) and a student\u2019s relative academic performance. Please consider these factors carefully if applying in 2024! <\/p>\n

Confirming Your Spot
\n<\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

Whether or not they are planning to attend, each student\u2019s parent\/guardian must fill out and submit the online Enrollment Form<\/strong>, which allows ATDP to confirm or release that space in the assigned course. This can be found in the FORMS tab of your online account. Please let us know as early as you can!\n<\/p>\n

Tuition Payment
\n<\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

Tuition payments are due by May 24 for those who applied by our standard deadline, and within 10 business days for applicants applying during our extended period. For families electing to pay in installments, a minimum amount of $100 of your tuition balance is due May 24, with the remaining balance to be paid in full no later than June 29.\n<\/p>\n

\nSee also our Course Placement & Wait Pool FAQs<\/strong><\/a>.\n<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

    Attending Class at Washington School <\/i><\/a><\/h1>\n
    \n
    \n
    \n

    Carpooling
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    For those interested in forming carpools, your account on the ATDP website will allow you to opt in to the commuter directory. Visit atdp.berkeley.edu\/account<\/a>, select \u201cCourse Placement,\u201d then click on the \u201cCommuter Info\u201d button next to your enrollment. Follow the instructions and select your preferences to find matches based on your course\u2019s meeting time, and on the areas through which you may be commuting. ED students also have the option of listing themselves for both AM and PM schedules if they have arranged for full-day activity. By opting in to ATDP\u2019s commuter directory, you agree to make your contact information available to other families using this service. ATDP is not liable for any circumstances resulting from the use of this information.<\/strong> The ATDP commuter directory is provided solely for your convenience. ATDP does not provide any other help or service in arranging for transportation to and from its program locations.\n<\/p>\n

    \nATDP\u2019s commuter directory is an automated service available only through our website. If you have questions or cannot access the commuter directory online, contact the ATDP office for assistance.\n<\/p>\n

    \nDropping Off and Picking Up Students
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    We will review drop-off and pick-up instructions during Orientation and have traffic monitors to guide you during the program. Please refer to this PDF<\/strong><\/a> for important instructions and procedures, noting the entry and exit gate (“Red Gate”) on East Richmond Avenue.\n<\/p>\n

    \nClass Schedule and Attendance
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    Classes will begin promptly at 8:30 a.m. or at 1:00 p.m. (depending on student\u2019s schedule) on Monday, July 10. Students should come to the first meeting prepared with a folder\/binder, writing implement, and a snack for recess. Other supplies will be provided. Prompt and regular attendance is required in all classes. Please make sure you\u2019re familiar with our attendance policy<\/a>.\n<\/p>\n

    In case of illness, a parent should call the Washington School office at 510-231-1417. The ATDP staff at Washington School phones home on a daily basis to confirm absences and discuss mitigation or other procedures. See our COVID safety page<\/strong><\/a> for up-to-date requirements.<\/strong>\n<\/p>\n

    Parents’ Space
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    For parents who wish to remain on campus while their children are in class, a parents\u2019 space may be available in the school library, subject to current public health and safety guidelines. As with all school visitors, parents wishing to use the library must sign in at the school office each day. They will be given a new visitor\u2019s badge every day they sign in.\n<\/p>\n

    Open House
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    Friday, July 28, during class (times vary). Most classes will open up their doors to families during part of the last day of class (usually the last hour). See class projects that students have worked hard on during the summer session. We will send you a schedule of events after classes begin.\n<\/p>\n

Registration information will be sent out during the summer program.\n<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

    \n

    \n

    Requesting a Change in Course Placement
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    All communications regarding changes in course selection or placement must be in writing and sent to the ATDP office through the online Modify Course Selection<\/strong> form. You can find links to this online form in the Course Placement tab of your online account<\/a>. You may only request courses for which you have not already been considered. You may also use this form to request to be added to the wait pool for a currently closed class or to request a second course (completing grade 4, 5, or 6); see below.\n<\/p>\n

    \nGive the Director at least ten business days to review your request and do not send more than one copy of your request. We often need to wait for spaces to open up before we can make course changes, so please be patient. We will make changes until the second week of June.\n<\/p>\n

    \nIf you are requesting a course change and are planning to decline your current course placement, please act promptly in submitting your Modify Course Selection<\/strong> form. We will send you a new course placement notice if we are able to accommodate your request. You will then have ten business days to accept or decline the new offer.\n<\/p>\n

    Requesting a second class (for grades 4, 5, and 6)<\/em><\/h5>\n

    Students in grades 4, 5 and 6 may apply for a second class and a full day at ATDP. Students taking two classes need to be ready for an entire day in academic classes. Alternatively, Cal Adventures Sport & Art Camp is an option that provides physical activity. Note: financial aid is not available for a second class.\n<\/p>\n

    Students request a second class by using the Modify Course Selection<\/strong> form. You can find links to this online form in the Course Placement tab of your online account<\/a>. Please see the course listings and current availability here on the ED catalog<\/a>.\n<\/p>\n

    Withdrawals & Refunds
    \n    <\/i><\/a><\/h4>\n

    If you decide not to accept your course placement, you must select the appropriate option on the online Enrollment Form (in the Forms tab of your account). The form must be submitted no later than Wednesday, May 24th, 2023, (Early and Standard window applicants) or within 2 weeks of your course placement offer (Extended window applicants), but please complete it as soon as possible so that another student can be placed in the class. If you need to withdraw after you have completed your Enrollment Form, please email your written withdrawal notice to ATDP.<\/p>\n

    Prior to the full refund deadline (June 7th, 2023), payments are refundable. After this date, refunds are subject to the Refund Schedule; see below. No refund will be made in the case of a student who fails to attend classes or is withdrawn from ATDP for failure to meet the standards of appropriate behavior, including completion of homework. Students with outstanding balances after June 29th, 2023 will be automatically withdrawn from the program without refund.\n<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n
